## On the Dao and all the Other Things I don't know Anything About
"[...]

It seems to me that all the most important life lessons; the ones which bear infinite repeating and relearning, are indeed almost infinitely repeated across human art and conversation. And discovering them, I have found, often feels more like a rediscovery than an epiphany. That even if the form is fresh and the details feel revelatory, some part of me basks in the comfort of having known all along. 

And though I could be regretful, and wish I had known them sooner, if instead, I am able to turn towards the possibilities they bring, I am left brimming with excitement, empathy, understanding and even pride in my past self. The person I can now see was grasping in the dark for precisely that truth. The person who in many senses and on many days, was already so very close to finding it. Who probably even managed for a time, before forgetting. Who, just like me in this very moment, though I may be using lofty words to give the appearance of the opposite, knows only a very small sliver of all the truths there are to be known.

I can only speak from my own experiences in this, and all regards, of course. But as I see it at least, there really is not much to worry yourself about on this front. Most of what you need to know to live your life to its fullest is within you already. And all the rest will be delivered to you endlessly from all angles and in all forms until you let it stick. You just have to want to hear it.
